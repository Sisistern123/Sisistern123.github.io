I'm Selin Türkoğlu, a Bioinformatics MSc student in the joint program of the
**Technical University of Munich (TUM)** and **Ludwig Maximilian University of
Munich (LMU)**, where I also completed my BSc. My work sits at the intersection
of **machine learning and deep learning** and the life sciences — I'm interested
in building models that turn biological data into useful predictions.

**Highlights**

* Co-authored a peer-reviewed paper in *Database* (2026) on venom-database
  metrics — uncommon at the Master's level.
* Research stay at the **Tsunoda Lab, University of Tokyo** (2026), on machine
  learning for anti-cancer drug-response prediction.
* Bachelor's thesis graded **1.0** (top mark) — animal toxin protein-family
  prediction (~98% accuracy with ProtT5 embeddings), now extended toward a publication.
* Hands-on with modern protein and genomic ML — ProtT5/ESM, scGPT, and genomic
  language models across several projects (see [Portfolio](/portfolio/)).

Right now (March–August 2026) I'm on a research stay at the
**Tsunoda Lab, University of Tokyo (Todai)**, working on **predicting anti-cancer
drug efficacy scores** — using machine learning to estimate how effective drugs
are likely to be.

Earlier, my bachelor's thesis tackled the **prediction of animal toxin protein
families**, and I co-authored my first paper, a quantitative analysis of growth
and diversification in venom databases (published in *Database*, 2026).

**Research interests:** machine &amp; deep learning for bioinformatics · protein
sequence modeling · drug response prediction · computational biology.

You can find my work on the [Publications](/publications/) and [Portfolio](/portfolio/)
pages, or take a look at my [CV](/cv/).
