A quantitative study of venom tissue data across two decades, using the Tox-Prot
database at three time points (2005, 2015, 2025). We show that while snakes,
spiders, cone snails, and scorpions continue to dominate, taxonomic and protein-family
diversity have grown substantially — with 503 new species and 188 new families
added by 2025 — and we characterize the representation biases that remain across
marine and terrestrial taxa.
